## 2024-05-13 - Securely Disabling Backups on Android 12+
**Vulnerability:** Application data could be extracted by unauthorized users during device-to-device transfers, even when `android:allowBackup="false"` is set.
**Learning:** In Android 12 (API 31) and higher, `android:allowBackup="false"` is ignored for device-to-device transfers. To securely prevent backup and transfer of sensitive data, developers must use `android:dataExtractionRules` and explicitly `<exclude>` all domains (root, file, database, sharedpref, external) under both `<cloud-backup>` and `<device-transfer>` in `data_extraction_rules.xml`, and apply the same `<exclude>` rules under `<full-backup-content>` in `backup_rules.xml`. Additionally, every `<exclude>` tag must explicitly declare both the `domain` and `path` attributes (e.g., `<exclude domain="root" path="." />`) to avoid XML syntax errors.
**Prevention:** Whenever sensitive data needs to be protected from extraction or device-to-device transfer, explicitly configure XML extraction and backup rules to exclude all data domains, in addition to setting `android:allowBackup="false"`.
